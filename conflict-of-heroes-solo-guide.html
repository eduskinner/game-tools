The Big Picture

What the solo game is, what's in both boxes, and how a solo firefight works

🎯 What Is This Game?

Conflict of Heroes is a tactical WWII wargame set at platoon-to-company scale on the Eastern Front in 1941. In solo mode, you command one side — Germans or Soviets — and the Athena AI (from the Eastern Front Solo Expansion) runs the other side automatically via a deck of Order Cards.

The core rhythm: you take an action with one of your units, then flip an Order Card to resolve both your Spent Check and the AI's next move. The result is a fluid, tense back-and-forth against an intelligent opponent you cannot fully predict.

WHAT THE SOLO EXPANSION ADDS

The Eastern Front Solo Expansion contains everything needed to play against the Athena AI:

• Order Cards — the heart of the solo system (AI brain + your Spent Check in one flip)
• Rumored Enemy (RE) Counters — fog-of-war placeholders for AI units you haven't confirmed yet
• Mission Track — a dynamic event timer that replaces fixed Rounds
• Solo Track Sheet — tracks VPs and CAPs for your solo session
• Solo Scenarios & Campaigns — dedicated solo firefights (the base game's 2-player missions are NOT directly playable solo without adaptation)

⚔️ Solo Firefight Structure

  • Setup — Place map, your units, AI units (and Rumored Enemy counters), CAP markers, Hit Marker pile, Mission Track
  • Your Turn: Take one action with a unit → flip an Order Card → resolve Spent Check → resolve AI action
  • Mission Track: Certain Order Card results advance the Mission Marker — triggering events and ending the firefight
  • Victory Points are tracked on the Solo Track Sheet — control objectives, destroy AI units
  • Firefight ends when the Mission Marker hits the last space — whoever has VP advantage wins
💡 KEY CONCEPT: FRESH vs. SPENT

Every unit is either Fresh (green bar face-up) or Spent (red bar face-up). Fresh units can always act. Spent units need CAPs to act. In solo mode, whether your unit becomes Spent is determined by flipping an Order Card — not by rolling a d10. Stats are identical on both sides.

Solo Game Flow

How a turn works in solo — your action, then the Order Card, then the AI reacts

🔄 The Solo Turn Sequence

Unlike the 2-player game where turns strictly alternate, in solo mode your turn and the AI's response are linked by a single Order Card flip. Here's the full flow:

YOUR STEP 1Select a Fresh Unit
YOUR STEP 2Perform an Action
SHARED STEP 3Flip Order Card
YOUR STEP 4Resolve Spent Check
AI STEP 5Resolve AI Action

The Order Card simultaneously tells you if your unit is Spent and what the AI does next. One card flip does double duty.

🃏 The Order Card — Your Central Tool

AGGRESSIVE
AI PRIORITY ORDER:
FIRE: Closest AI to a Unit → Short Range attack
MOVE: Closest AI → Move towards Unit
IF NONE: AI Passes
AP SPENT # : 4

Reading an Order Card

HEADER AI personality for this card (Aggressive, Defensive, etc.) — some headers are blue, meaning the AI unit activated does NOT make a Spent Check
BODY Priority list of actions the AI will attempt, in order — work down the list until one is possible
AP # BOTTOM The AP Spent Number — compare your unit's action cost to this to determine if YOUR unit is Spent
HOW YOUR SPENT CHECK WORKS IN SOLO

After you act, look at the AP Spent Number at the bottom of the flipped Order Card.

• If your Action Cost ≥ AP Spent Number → your unit becomes Spent (flip to red bar side)
• If your Action Cost < AP Spent Number → your unit stays Fresh

Example: You move a unit (1AP). The Order Card shows AP Spent # 4. Since 1 < 4, your unit stays Fresh. But if you attacked with a 4AP action and the card shows AP Spent # 4, then 4 ≥ 4 → Spent!

You can spend CAPs to lower your action cost before acting, reducing the chance of becoming Spent — just as in the 2-player game.

🤖 How the Athena AI Takes Its Turn

After your Spent Check, the Order Card body tells the AI what to do. The AI always works through the priority list on the card from top to bottom, taking the first action it can legally perform:

  • The AI selects a unit based on the card's criteria (e.g., "closest AI unit to a player unit")
  • It attempts the first listed action (usually Fire or Move); if impossible, it tries the next one
  • The AI evaluates the actual board state — it fires at real targets, seeks cover, flanks when possible
  • If the AI unit's card header is blue, it does NOT make a Spent Check after acting
  • Some cards advance the Mission Marker 1, 2, or 3 spaces — triggering events or ending the firefight
RUMORED ENEMY (RE) COUNTERS

Fog of war: RE counters are placed where AI units might be. As you move into range or trigger reveals, RE counters are replaced by actual AI unit counters. This creates genuine uncertainty — you don't always know exactly what or where the enemy is!

✋ Passing in Solo

You may Pass instead of acting. When you Pass, you still flip an Order Card — the AI still gets its action. Passing removes Stress from your units but does not "end the round" by itself; the Mission Track drives the game's pacing instead of consecutive passes.

Scenario 1 — Your First Solo Turn

Setup: Place 1 German Rifles unit (yours) facing north on the map. Place 1 Soviet Rifles unit (AI) 4 hexes ahead. Give yourself 7 CAPs.

YOUR TURN

Move your German Rifles 1 hex north (Move Cost = 1AP). Place a Stress marker on the unit.

FLIP ORDER CARD

Look at the AP Spent Number at the bottom. If it's 2 or higher (e.g., shows "3"), 1 < 3 → unit stays Fresh! If it shows "1", then 1 ≥ 1 → unit becomes Spent.

AI REACTS

Read the card body. If the top priority says "Fire on closest player unit" — the Soviet Rifles check if the German unit is in their Fire Zone. If yes, they attack. Work through the combat rules from that point.

LESSON

Notice how one card does two things: checked your fate AND drove the AI. This is the core rhythm of solo play. After a few turns it becomes intuitive.

Order Cards & Spent Check (Deep Dive)

Understanding probability, Stress, CAP spending, and how to read AI priorities

📊 Action Cost vs. AP Spent Number

The AP Spent Number on Order Cards replaces the d10 die roll from the 2-player rules. The principle is the same: higher-cost actions are riskier. The card deck is calibrated so that on average, units become Spent at roughly the same rate as in the 2-player game.

Your Action CostBecomes Spent when card shows AP# of…Approx. Risk
1AP (Move)1 or lowerLow risk
2AP (MG Attack)2 or lowerModerate
3AP (Rifles Attack)3 or lower~50/50
4AP (Soviet Attack)4 or lowerHigher risk
5AP (Rally)5 or lowerVery likely Spent
SPENDING CAPs TO REDUCE ACTION COST (SOLO RULE)

Before acting, you may spend any number of CAPs to reduce your Action Cost by 1 per CAP. This lowers the chance of becoming Spent, exactly as in the 2-player game. A 3AP attack reduced to 1AP with 2 CAPs only becomes Spent if the card shows AP# 1 or lower — much safer!

Reducing cost to 0AP: no Spent Check occurs at all. Even a Spent unit can act at 0AP!

⚠️ Stress

Stress works the same as in the 2-player game: when a unit takes an action, place a Stress marker on it. Its next action costs +1AP extra (which also raises its Spent risk).

STRESS = +1AP TO ACTION COST

A unit that moves (1AP) and is Stressed will cost 2AP to act again. That means it becomes Spent if the Order Card shows AP# 2 or lower — significantly worse odds. Alternate between units to manage Stress.

Stress markers are removed when you Pass (and the AI still acts via Order Card) or at the start of the next firefight phase (consult your solo scenario for timing).

🤖 Reading AI Priority Lists

The body of each Order Card is a priority list. Work from top to bottom and execute the first possible action:

  • FIRE orders: AI selects the specified unit (e.g., "AI unit closest to a player unit") and attacks if the target is in its Fire Zone. Uses the same combat rules as your attacks.
  • MOVE orders: AI moves the specified unit toward a target (e.g., "towards Cover", "towards a player Unit"). It always takes the most efficient legal path.
  • PASS: If no listed action is possible, the AI Passes for this activation.
  • Risk levels: Some cards specify "Low Risk", "Medium Risk" or "High Risk" — this affects whether the AI will move into exposed positions or take aggressive close-combat actions.
BLUE-HEADER ORDER CARDS

When an Order Card has a blue header and is used to activate an AI unit, that AI unit does NOT make a Spent Check. It stays Fresh regardless of what action it took. These cards make the AI especially potent — multiple AI units can stay active longer.

Scenario 2 — Stress & CAP Practice

Setup: Your German MG34 (2AP attack) faces Soviet Rifles 2 hexes north. You have 4 CAPs. MG34 already has a Stress marker from last turn.

STRESSED ACTION

MG34 wants to attack: 2AP base + 1AP Stress = 3AP total Action Cost.

USE CAPs

Spend 1 CAP to reduce to 2AP. Now the MG34 only becomes Spent if the Order Card shows AP# 2 or lower.

FLIP ORDER CARD

Say the card shows AP# 4. Your 2AP action cost < 4 → MG34 stays Fresh! Then resolve the AI's action from the card body.

EXPERIMENT

Now try NOT spending the CAP (3AP cost vs. the same AP# 4 card). 3 < 4 → still Fresh! Sometimes CAPs aren't needed. Save them for when it really matters.

Movement & the Map

How your units move, facing, terrain costs, and controlling objectives

🧭 Facing — The Green Bar

Every unit has a green bar on one side — that's where it's "looking." A unit must face one of the six sides of its hex. Facing determines your Arc of Fire (where you can shoot) and whether attacks against you use your stronger Front Defense or weaker Flank Defense.

FRONT HEXES (3 hexes)

The hex directly ahead + the two front-diagonal hexes. These are in your Arc of Fire — where you can shoot.

FLANK HEXES (3 hexes)

The hex behind you + two rear-diagonal hexes. The AI will try to flank you — attacks from here use your lower Flank Defense!

THE AI FLANKS — WATCH YOUR FACING

Certain Order Cards instruct the AI to "Move to Flank" a player unit before firing. This is why positioning and facing are critical in solo play. Always check whether your units' Flank hexes are exposed to AI units that might be activated next.

👟 Foot Unit Movement

On a Move Action, a Foot Unit moves from its current hex to any adjacent hex (1 hex). After moving, it may freely pivot to face any direction — at no extra cost. It cannot pivot before moving, only after. Pivoting in Place (no movement) also costs 1AP.

BACKWARDS MOVE: +1AP PENALTY

Moving into one of your own Flank hexes costs +1AP extra. This penalty stacks with terrain and Stress penalties.

🏁 Control Markers (Objectives)

Control Markers flip to your side when: (1) the current controlling side has no units in the hex, AND (2) a unit with positive Firepower moves into it. In solo mode, the AI also tries to contest objectives — watch the Order Cards for "Move Towards Objective" instructions.

Scenario 3 — Navigate the Terrain Solo

Setup: Your German Rifles unit at the south edge of Map 1. A Soviet RE counter (Rumored Enemy) is placed 5 hexes north in Light Woods. A Control Marker (Soviet side up) is in Light Woods 3 hexes north. You have 7 CAPs.

YOUR TURN 1

Move into an Open hex: 1AP. Flip Order Card → resolve Spent Check → resolve AI action (probably the RE "enemy" patrols or moves).

YOUR TURN 2

Move into the Light Woods Control Marker hex: 1AP base + 1AP Difficult Terrain + 1AP Stress = 3AP. Flip Order Card — will you be Spent?

CONTROL

You're now in the objective hex. Flip Control Marker to German side — score a VP at mission end!

AI REVEAL

If the Order Card's AI action says "Fire" and the RE counter is now in range, the RE counter is replaced by the actual AI unit and it fires on you. This is how hidden AI units are revealed in solo play!

The Fire Zone

Arc of Fire, Line of Sight, and Range — three checks before every attack

🎯 Can I Attack That Hex? Three Conditions

THE FIRE ZONE CHECKLIST (applies to YOU and the AI)

✅ 1. Target is within your Arc of Fire (your 3 front hexes extended across the map)
✅ 2. Target is within your Line of Sight (no blocking terrain between you)
✅ 3. Target is within twice your Range (beyond Range = −2AR Long Range Penalty)

The AI uses these exact same rules. If you're out of an AI unit's Arc of Fire, it will be instructed to pivot or move before firing. Pay attention!

🔭 Arc of Fire

Take the 3 Front hexes of a unit and extend them radially across the whole map like a cone. The 3 Flank hexes are NOT in the Arc of Fire — no unit can fire behind itself without pivoting first.

👁️ Line of Sight (LOS)

Draw a line from the center of your hex to the center of the target hex. If this line crosses any of the following terrain hexes, LOS is blocked:

  • Light or Heavy Woods
  • Wood or Stone Buildings
KEY LOS RULES

• All units (yours AND AI) do NOT block LOS — only terrain does.
• A unit CAN shoot at an enemy in a woods/building hex — only what's BETWEEN them matters.
• LOS is reversible: if you can see the AI, it can see you.
• Terrain type is determined by the hex's center dot only.

📏 Range & Range Modifiers

Range is printed in yellow at the bottom center of the counter. Count hexes from attacker to target (not counting attacker's hex, but counting target's hex).

Normal Range+0 AR
Long Range (>Range, ≤2×Range)−2AR
Short Range (adjacent hex)+3AR
Close Combat (same hex)+4AR

Scenario 4 — Fire Zone Check (Solo)

Setup: Your German MG34 faces north (Range 4). Two AI units: (A) Soviet Rifles 3 hexes north, (B) Soviet Rifles 2 hexes east. Light Woods hex is 2 hexes north between you and A.

CHECK SOVIET A

3 hexes north: Arc ✓ (directly ahead). LOS: is the Light Woods hex in between? If yes → BLOCKED. Cannot attack without moving to a better position!

CHECK SOVIET B

2 hexes east: Check Arc of Fire. Facing north, your Flank hexes are to the east. NOT IN ARC → Cannot attack. Must pivot first (costs 1AP Move action).

NOW THE AI CHECKS

Flip an Order Card. It says "Fire on closest player unit." Soviet A must check its own Fire Zone against your MG34. If the LOS is blocked both ways, the AI will instead get a "Move" order on the next card.

LESSON

Positioning is a two-way street in solo play — your LOS rules and the AI's LOS rules are identical. Use terrain to block AI line of sight to your units!

Combat

The DR vs AR formula — identical for you and the AI, every attack every time

💥 The Attack Formula

All combat — yours or the AI's — uses this same system:

Hit Number = Defense Rating (DR) − Attack Rating (AR)
Roll 2d6 ≥ Hit Number → HIT!  |  Roll 2d6 < Hit Number → Miss
Roll 4+ above Hit Number → CRITICAL HIT (instant destroy!)

In solo play, you roll the 2d6 for both your attacks AND the AI's attacks. The same fair dice decide everything.

🛡️ Defense Rating (DR)

DR = Target's base Defense + Terrain modifier

BASE DEFENSE (bottom right of counter)

Front Defense (higher) — attacked from within Arc of Fire.
Flank Defense (lower) — attacked from outside Arc of Fire.

TERRAIN DR MODIFIERS

Light Woods: +1DR   Heavy Woods: +2DR
Wood Building: +1DR   Stone Building: +2DR
Walls: +1DR   Water: −1DR

SOFT vs ARMORED TARGETS

Red Defense = Soft Target (infantry, guns). Blue Defense = Armored (tanks). Attacker uses matching colored Firepower.

⚡ Attack Rating (AR)

AR = Attacker's Firepower + all applicable modifiers:

Short Range (adjacent)+3AR
Long Range (beyond Range)−2AR
Close Combat (same hex)+4AR
High Elevation vs Low+1AR attacker
Low vs High Elevation+1DR defender
Flank AttackUse Flank Defense!
YOU ROLL FOR AI ATTACKS TOO

When an Order Card directs an AI unit to fire, you calculate its Hit Number (same formula), then roll 2d6. If the result ≥ Hit Number, your unit is hit. Draw a Hit Marker from the pile and place it face-down under your unit. The AI doesn't "know" what the hit marker does — it doesn't change strategy based on it. Only you can look at it!

📊 Worked Example (Solo)

Your German MG34 (4 Red FP, Range 4) attacks a Soviet Rifles AI unit in Light Woods, at normal range, from the front:

DR: 12 Front Def + 1 Light Woods = 13DR
AR: 4 FP + 0 Range = 4AR
Hit Number: 13 − 4 = 9  (need 2d6 ≥ 9)
Critical Hit if roll ≥ 13. Spend up to 2 CAPs to lower Hit Number before rolling.

Scenario 5 — Your First Solo Combat

Setup: Your German MG34 faces north. Soviet Rifles AI unit 2 hexes ahead in Open terrain. You have 5 CAPs.

ATTACK ACTION

Declare attack on Soviet AI unit. Verify Fire Zone ✓. DR: 12 Front, Open: +0 = 12DR. AR: 4 FP + 3 Short Range = 7AR. Hit Number: 12−7 = 5.

SPEND CAPs?

Spend 1 CAP to lower Hit Number to 4. Now 2d6 ≥ 4 — very high chance of hitting! Roll the dice.

FLIP ORDER CARD

AP Spent Number says "3". MG34's Attack Cost is 2AP (you spent 1 CAP). 2 < 3 → MG34 stays Fresh. Then read the AI action — maybe the Soviets now try to return fire or move to cover.

AI FIRES BACK (if ordered)

If the Order Card directs the AI Rifles to fire at your MG34: DR (MG34 in Open) = 10 Front. AR: 3 FP + 3 Short Range = 6AR. Hit Number: 10−6 = 4. You roll for the AI — same 2d6.

Hits & Rallying

Secret Hit Markers for both sides — and how to recover your battered units

💀 The Hit System

When any attack succeeds, the target draws a Hit Marker face-down from the appropriate pile and slides it under the unit. In solo, this works the same for your units and for AI units — both sides have hidden hit states.

SECOND HIT = DESTROYED

If a unit with a Hit Marker is hit again, it's immediately destroyed — removed from the map and placed on the CAPs Track (permanently reducing your future CAP allocation per destroyed unit).

AI HIT MARKERS — SOLO RULE

When your attack hits an AI unit, that AI unit also draws a face-down Hit Marker. In solo play, you do NOT look at the AI's Hit Marker (it's genuinely hidden information). The Hit Marker's effect on the AI's stats is only revealed when that stat is used in combat — just as in the 2-player game. This preserves genuine fog of war even when you're the only human at the table!

🩹 Rallying Your Units

A Hit unit may attempt a Rally Action to remove its Hit Marker. Costs 5AP (plus Stress if applicable).

Roll 2d6 ≥ Rally Number (on Hit Marker) → Hit Marker removed!

Lower the Rally Number by (cumulative):

  • Light/Heavy Woods, Wood/Stone Buildings, Walls: −1 each
  • Heavy Smoke in any terrain: −1
  • Each friendly un-hit unit in same hex: −1 per unit
  • Spending CAPs (up to 2): −1 per CAP
AI RALLY — SOLO RULE

The AI does not rally on its own initiative. AI units can only rally if an Order Card specifically directs a Rally action. This is intentional — it means you can exploit hit AI units before they recover if you act quickly enough!

Scenario 6 — Hit & Rally in Solo

Your Soviet Rifles (you play Soviet side this time) have been hit — Suppressed marker face-down. You have 6 CAPs. The German AI Rifles are 3 hexes away.

AI FIRES AGAIN

Flip Order Card — AI is directed to fire. The Suppressed Hit Marker is now revealed (affects Firepower −2). Calculate the AI's attack. If Hit Number is met, your unit is destroyed!

YOUR RALLY ATTEMPT

Use your turn to rally instead: 5AP cost. Suppressed Rally # = 7. In Light Woods: −1. Spend 1 CAP: −1 more. Need 2d6 ≥ 5. Flip Order Card (for Spent Check). If you roll ≥ 5 → Suppressed removed!

AFTER YOUR RALLY

Flip Order Card to check Spent (5AP Rally vs AP# on card). Then the AI acts — it might try to advance or fire at a different unit while your Rifles recover.

LESSON

In solo, Hit units are vulnerable. The AI doesn't wait for you to recover — it will try to press the advantage with the next card. Rally quickly or use CAPs to reduce rally cost!

Command Action Points (Solo)

Your most precious resource — how to use them and why protecting them is everything

⭐ CAPs in Solo Play

CAPs work identically to the 2-player game — they represent your force's leadership and logistics. You receive a fixed number each mission phase (set by the scenario). The AI does NOT have CAPs; it acts based purely on Order Cards.

CAPs ARE YOUR EDGE OVER THE AI

The AI gets no CAPs — it can't modify dice rolls or reactivate Spent units. Your CAPs are your primary advantage. Use them wisely to tip key combats in your favor and keep critical units active when the AI puts pressure on you.

DESTROYED UNITS REDUCE YOUR FUTURE CAPs

Each of your destroyed units permanently reduces your starting CAPs by 1 (never below 3). The AI feels this pain through you — every unit you lose makes future turns harder. Protect your units like they carry your command capacity, because they do.

🧠 Solo CAP Strategy

  • Spend 1–2 CAPs to lower your Hit Number when attacking a key AI unit — turning a 50/50 into 80/20 is worth it
  • If a critical unit is Spent and the AI is about to fire on it, spend CAPs to rally it or move it out of danger
  • Don't burn CAPs on trivial actions — a 1AP move rarely needs CAP protection
  • Remember: the AI doesn't benefit from CAPs, so any CAP spend is a pure advantage for you
  • Events on the Mission Track can drain your CAPs — keep a reserve for emergencies

Scenario 7 — The Critical CAP Decision

Your German MG34 (2AP attack, Stressed) is facing a key AI target. You have only 3 CAPs left. The AI Soviet unit is in a Stone Building (huge defense bonus).

CALCULATE HIT NUMBER

Soviet: 12 Front Def + 2 Stone Building = 14DR. MG34: 4 FP + 3 Short Range (adjacent) = 7AR. Hit Number: 14−7 = 7. Plus Stress: attack cost is 2+1 = 3AP.

OPTION A — Spend 2 CAPs on Attack

Lower Hit Number from 7 to 5. Need 2d6 ≥ 5 (83% hit chance). That uses 2 of 3 CAPs. Attack Cost = 3AP (Stressed).

OPTION B — Spend 1 CAP on Attack + 1 on Spent

Hit Number → 6 (72% hit). AND lower Attack Cost 3AP → 2AP before Order Card comparison. Safer on both fronts, but less attack power.

OPTION C — Save All CAPs

Take the 7 Hit Number (58% hit). Keep 3 CAPs for later. What if you'll need to rally a hit unit next turn?

MISSION TRACK WARNING

Remember: Mission Track events can drain your CAPs. If the next Order Card advances the Mission Marker onto a "Lose 2 CAPs" event, you might regret spending them now!

Mission Track & Battle Cards

How solo firefights end, Mission Track events, Victory Points, and your Battle Cards

⏱️ The Mission Track — How Firefights End

In solo mode, there are no fixed Rounds. Instead, each firefight has a Mission Track — a series of spaces, some with events. When certain Order Cards are resolved, the Mission Marker advances 1, 2, or 3 spaces along the track.

MISSION TRACK REPLACES ROUNDS

• The firefight ends immediately when the Mission Marker lands on or passes the last space of the Mission Track.
• Along the way, if the Marker lands on an Event space, resolve that event immediately.
• Events can include: losing CAPs, an AI unit activating as a bonus, Rumored Enemy appearing, or other scenario-specific effects.
• Because card draws are random, the length of each firefight varies — this creates genuine replayability and tension!

🏆 Victory Points (Solo)

VPs are tracked on your Solo Track Sheet. You earn VPs by:

  • Destroying AI units
  • Controlling objective hexes (Control Markers) when scored
  • Achieving specific mission objectives listed in the solo scenario

The scenario specifies what score you need for a win, partial victory, or loss. Unlike the 2-player game's "VP Advantage" track, solo scoring is simpler — you hit a target or you don't.

🃏 Battle Cards (Your Solo Tactical Edge)

You have access to the same Battle Cards as in the 2-player game. Each Mission specifies which numbered Battle Cards make up your hand. You start with Card #01 (Adrenaline) plus draw additional cards as specified.

THE AI HAS NO BATTLE CARDS

Battle Cards are exclusively your tool. The AI operates entirely through Order Cards. This is a significant asymmetric advantage — use your cards aggressively to create the decisive moments the Order Cards won't give the AI.

🔄 Phase Reset (Between Firefight Phases)

Solo scenarios may divide play into phases rather than rounds. At the end of a phase (when the Mission Track triggers a phase change), typically:

PHASE RESET (check your solo scenario for exact sequence)
Flip all Spent units → Fresh  |  Reset CAPs (−1 per destroyed unit)  |  Draw Battle Cards
Resolve any phase-start events  |  Check reinforcement schedule  |  Continue Mission Track

Scenario 8 — Play Your First Solo Firefight!

You're now ready to tackle Solo Mission 1. Here's how to approach it:

SETUP

Follow the solo scenario's setup exactly — place your units, AI units (or RE counters), Control Markers, and the Mission Track. Note your starting CAPs and Battle Cards.

RHYTHM

Your action → flip Order Card → Spent Check → AI action → check Mission Marker. Repeat. After 3–4 turns this becomes automatic.

AI CONFUSION?

If an Order Card's instructions seem ambiguous (e.g., "closest AI to a Unit" when there are ties), always resolve in the way that is hardest for you. The AI is meant to be a challenging opponent.

AFTER THE FIREFIGHT

Reflect: Did you manage CAPs well? Did you use Battle Cards at the right moments? Did terrain save you or hurt you? Solo play rewards replaying the same scenario multiple times to improve your score!

Advanced Rules Overview

Hills, mortars, vehicles, fortifications — and how the Athena AI handles them all

🕵️ Rumored Enemy — The Fog of War System

RE counters are placed on the map at setup (and sometimes added via Mission Track events) to represent possible AI unit positions you haven't confirmed. You see the counters but don't know if they're real until revealed.

HOW RE COUNTERS WORK

• RE counters on the map represent suspected AI positions — they may or may not be actual units.
• When an Order Card activates an AI unit and you check whether an RE counter is real, follow the solo scenario's reveal rules.
• When a real AI unit is revealed, replace the RE counter with the actual unit counter facing toward you.
• Your combat against unrevealed positions is resolved as "Recon by Fire" — you might reveal a hidden unit AND attack it in one roll.

⛰️ Hills & Elevation

ELEVATION BASICS

Hexes can be Level 0 (ground), Level 1 Hill, or Level 2 Hill. Moving uphill costs foot units +1AP per level. The AI follows these exact same rules — Order Cards directing the AI to move uphill cost more and risk spending the AI unit (the AI has no CAPs to mitigate this, which is a genuine advantage for you on hilly maps!). High ground gives +1AR to attacker, +1DR to defender.

💣 Mortars & Artillery

Mortars can fire directly (line-of-sight) or indirectly over obstacles using a Spotter Hex. Artillery cards give Off-Board Artillery — planned one phase, fired in the next, subject to drift. The AI also uses mortar and artillery units — Order Cards will direct them to fire. Remember:

HE (HIGH EXPLOSIVE) ALWAYS HITS FLANK DEFENSE

Mortar and artillery attacks always resolve against the target's Flank Defense — even if attacking from the front. In Heavy Woods, soft targets get no +2DR woods bonus (air burst). This makes HE weapons devastatingly effective against entrenched infantry on both sides.

🚗 Vehicles

Vehicles have Bonus Moves — extra hexes per Move action. Key solo vehicle notes:

  • Tracked (blue Move Cost): Bonus Moves on roads and Open terrain
  • Wheeled (green Move Cost): Bonus Moves on Roads only
  • Turreted: fire 360° at +2AP cost outside Arc of Fire
  • The AI vehicle will follow Order Card movement instructions exactly — if told to "Move Towards Cover", it picks the nearest cover regardless of movement cost
  • AI vehicles do NOT receive Terrain defensive bonuses in Close Combat — exploit this when using your infantry against enemy tanks!
  • Armored Hit Markers include Immobilized, Gun Damaged, Suppressed, Destroyed — some cannot be Rallied (physical damage)

🏰 Fortifications & Obstacles

BUNKERS (+5DR front, +3DR flank)

Extremely tough to assault. If the AI occupies a bunker, it becomes very hard to dislodge — plan your attacks with HE mortars or flamethrowers.

TRENCHES (omnidirectional DR)

Protection from any direction. Both your units and AI units benefit. In solo scenarios, AI units often start in trenches — you must bring enough firepower to overcome the defense bonus.

MINES & BARBED WIRE

In solo scenarios, mines can be placed by either side (check scenario rules). When your unit triggers a mine, you roll against the mine's Hit Number — it's an automatic attack with no AR, just a Hit Number. Pioneers can enter mine hexes safely. Tracked vehicles destroy wire by driving through it.

🔥 Flamethrowers

Pioneers and certain tanks have flamethrowers. Always 3 Red + 3 Blue FP, max Range 1. Always attacks Flank Defense, ignores ALL terrain DR bonuses (except Smoke). In solo mode, flamethrowers are your best tool for clearing bunkers and buildings that the AI is using for cover — the AI's terrain bonus advantage disappears completely.

🗺️ Solo Campaign Structure

The Eastern Front Solo Expansion includes two full campaigns:

CAMPAIGN 1 — GERMAN 10TH PANZER DIVISION

You command elements of the 10th Panzer Division during the 1941 advance on Moscow. Build your forces across multiple linked firefights — your surviving units carry over, with persistent hit markers and reduced CAPs from cumulative losses.

CAMPAIGN 2 — SOVIET 32ND ARMY

Switch sides! Command Soviet forces trying to undo the German gains from Campaign 1. You're playing against the same maps but from the other direction — a completely fresh strategic challenge.

Between campaigns, you can also generate standalone firefights using the Firefight Generator (sold separately), which integrates with the Solo Expansion to create custom scenarios with unique solo command cards.

Your Solo Learning Path

SOLO FIREFIGHTS 1–2

Core rules only: moving, attacking, rallying, CAPs, Order Cards, Mission Track. Master the card-flip rhythm before adding complexity.

AI MASTERY TIP

When the Order Card instructions feel unclear, always resolve them in the way that creates the hardest opponent for you. The Athena AI is designed to be challenging — don't give it the benefit of generous interpretations.

SOLO FIREFIGHTS 3–4

Add Hills, Elevation, and Mortars. Elevation changes how you think about positioning on both sides of the board.

SOLO FIREFIGHTS 5–6

Full vehicle rules — tanks, transport, armored combat. The AI handles vehicles well via Order Cards.

CAMPAIGNS

Once comfortable with individual firefights, start Campaign 1. Persistent losses across missions create long-term strategic pressure that single firefights don't capture.

🏅 SOLO COMMANDER'S CREED

The Athena AI has no CAPs — you do. The AI has no Battle Cards — you do. But the AI is relentless, doesn't panic, and follows optimal Order Card logic every single turn. Your edge is flexibility, resource management, and creativity. Use CAPs at decisive moments. Play Battle Cards aggressively. And always, always respect the terrain — it's your best weapon against an enemy that never gets tired.
